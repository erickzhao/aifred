Management

Abhishek Gupta
Project Lead

Abhishek Gupta is the Project Lead for aifred, a McGill Prestige Scholar with a degree in Computer Science, Abhishek co-founded aifred with an aim to bring personalized treatments in psychiatry at scale by combining best practices in psychiatry and machine learning.
An entrepreneur and inventor at heart, he has honed his skills on many technical projects. Through his experience at the Identity & Trust division in the Chief Innovation Office at Ericsson he has gained experience in cybersecurity and blockchain technology. An avid reader and writer, his works have been featured in several publications including The Next Web.
He’s been invited by the United Nations on multiple occasions to share his work in previous projects and as an active member of the Montreal startup ecosystem he has given back to the community by sharing valuable lessons learned along the way. Most recently he was invited by the UN and the XPrize Foundation to the global AI for Good summit where he shared two sets of principles on the ethical and equitable distribution of AI technologies as developed at aifred - “meticulous transparency” and “democratization of AI”. Details are available on request.

David Benrimoh, MD
Psychiatry Expert and Machine Learning Research

David is a physician currently training to be a psychiatrist. He is also pursuing a Masters in Neuroscience at University College London as well as neuroimaging research at McGill. He obtained his MD.,CM from McGill. At aifred he serves as the Psychiatry Content Expert and as a Technical Team Member, acting as the bridge between the Research and Machine Learning Teams.

Kelly Perlman
Research Manager

Kelly Perlman is a co-founder of aifred. She has the primary goal of ensuring that every step in the development of aifred is firmly grounded in science in order to identify strong, accurate, and relevant predictors of treatment response. Kelly is a neuroscience student at McGill University, and has been working in a neuroimaging laboratory since 2014, where she has gained technical skills and research experience. Additionally, she is the VP external of McGill’s neurotechnology club (MENTAL), and is immensely passionate about all things science.

Robert Fratila
Machine-Learning and Technology Manager

Robert Fratila is the Manager for the Machine Learning and Technology division at aifred. He is very passionate about combining medicine and AI throughout his work and research. His work as a software developer at the Montreal Neurological Institute, integrating state-of-the-art machine learning models in health care, specifically brain imaging, has given him lots of experience in finding efficient solutions to complex problems.
In his spare time, he has worked on several projects including a search engine, a brain-state classifier, full computer vision packages for autonomous underwater vehicles and a program that creates a predictive model for future patients diagnosed with Leukemia, just to name a few.
He has dedicated much of his time to giving workshops and talks about his work to student groups at McGill including the Neuroscience Undergraduates of McGill (NUM) and McGill Women in Computer Science (McWiCS). He is commited to spreading knowledge and demystifying the wonderful field of machine learning.

Eunice You
Researcher

Eunice You is a medical student at McGill University who is passionate by research. Over the years, she has pursued various projects ranging from environmental field studies to stem cell engineering, 3D-printing for bone scaffolds to bacterial microbots for tumour-targeting. Her research has been presented at several international science competitions and garnered many prestigious awards. She has also interned at the Montreal Neurological Institute and the Douglas Mental Health University Institute and served as Vice-President NeuroSymposium.
Being involved in research from a young age also heightened her desire to give back to her community. She is involved in several student initiatives, from founding a hospital volunteering club to her work on the student-led outreach program BioMed Network.

Nykan Mirchi
Product Developer

Nykan is currently completing a bachelor in biochemistry and neuroscience at McGill University. He has developed considerable programming and research experience in the field of psychiatry and neuroscience. Some of his most notable work include his research on the cognitive and physiological effects of marijuana at UCLA, in addition to a study of brain networks in mood disorders at the Montreal Neurological Institute.
